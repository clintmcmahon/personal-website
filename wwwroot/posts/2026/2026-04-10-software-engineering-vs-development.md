I approach software development/engineering from a few different angles. Software engineering isn't just about writing code. I equate that closer to software development now. Three years ago I would have been annoyed with anyone trying to say they were different things. With AI agents and assisted coding I can really see a difference in software engineering, architecture, and software development.

Because of AI I see a different between software engineering and software development. Software development is the act of coding preexisting requirements to build a solution. Software engineering is like a step up, it still encasulates development, but includes the high level application design, requirements, planning and system design on top of development. The difference is someone who puts together the entire application in their head before handing off the jobs to ther person to put the peices together. 

AI models are excellent at coding solutions that have concrete plans put in front of them. If the engineer is good at problem definition and solution design, they will be able to design the system so an AI agent can code it for them. The AI agent isn't going to understand business problems or nuanced requirements if the engineer does not explicitly define them. Just like a junior engineer is not going to understand business rules without guidance. 

There's a greater focus on letting the AI systems do the coding — that mental process of typing syntax into the IDE - and having us humans plan what the code is going to accomplish. Designing the system or code that will become your controller, component, or feature is now the most important part. You tell an agent what the end results should be and how to get there. Then let the agent write the code. 

But has that really changed in the past 20 years?  Yes and no. In my 20+ years of software engineering I've lways spent more time on requirements planning and system design than I have at actually coding things. So much of software development and engineering is defining the problem and solution. Once you figure out those two things, coding the solution is easy part. So in a sense, that's hows it's always been. But instead of the engineer or architect passing the requieremtns down to a junior developer, that architect can have the agent do the building instead of another developer on the team.
