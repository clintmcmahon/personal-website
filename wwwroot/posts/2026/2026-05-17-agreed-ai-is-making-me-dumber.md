<a href="https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48139148">God Damn AI is making me dumb</a> via HN. I agree with this blog post by James Pain so much. The more I use AI, the more I rely on it instead of thinking for myself. It's easy to offload my brain activity to this little answer machine to do my thinking.

**Maps**

At one point, maps were making me dumber with directions and getting around town. Instead of thinking about the route, I'd just load the map on my wife's phone and blindly follow the route. At some point my wife said "Let's not use our phone, let's use our brains." This is a thing we say now. Whenever we feel like we're using maps for directions or when there seems to be a good reason to think, we will say "put the phone down, let's use our brains." This is good on multiple levels. We explore more instead of a direct A to B, there's more exploring involved as we move through the city. We have discovered more along the way that we never would have using the app. Better still, it creates and reinforces our mental map of the city. I do feel that using the maps on all our phones makes us dumber in that I forget how to get around a city I've lived in for 20 years.

To overcome this slippery slope I really think about solution architecture now since I don't have to worry about coding -- how will the code work, how to develop this feature. That part is solved now. **Writing**

This is the big one for me. I've never been a strong writer or speaker. But with AI, when there is something I want to say in a blog post or a random email, I can just ask Claude to write it for me. <!-- REVIEW: "the robot sounds like the robot and not like me as I wrote it" is unclear. I want my posts to make sense so I will have AI read my writing and tell me the holes that need to be filled so that a new reader can clearly understand the post. It makes it so that I want to do the thinking but it suggests what and where the improvements need to be made.
